Resarch Projects

Circuit Design of Posit Arithmetic

Posit was proposed to replace IEEE-754 standards; however, due to extensive hardware consumption, Posit format is not yet accepted in general-purpose processors. We are building a new architecture design for Posit to reduce its hardware cost.

Posit-Quantisation for DNNs

The quantisation is a prominent method to reduce bitwidth in neural networks. Since Posit has a higher dynamic range as compared to IEEE-754, we can apply Posit-quantisation in neural networks to minimise arithmetic unit size as well as memory requirement to store its weights.

Framework for Evaluation and Design for Approximate Circuits

We have proposed a framework, exploiting CAD tools, to generate various approximate designs from a netlist. Pareto optimality is used to choose the most desirable approximate design for a user-specific application.

Other Projects

Edge-NILM (Non-Intrusive Load Monitoring on Edge-Devices)

Neural Networks are computationally and memory intensive, therefore not suitable for edge devices. Various compression techniques are explored to understand their impact on load-monitoring neural network models. We have also proposed a multi-task learning-based architecture to compress the models further.
